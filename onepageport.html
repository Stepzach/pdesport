Section 00 — About 00

Design
Philosophy

I approach product design as a practice of constraint — the belief that meaningful objects and interfaces emerge not from unlimited possibility, but from the careful negotiation of human need, material truth, and technological reality.

My work spans physical product and digital interface, often exploring the boundary between them. I am drawn to systems that demand legibility: things that explain themselves through their form, that do not require a manual.

Forma

Wearable Tech Health Physical Product

Forma addresses a persistent tension in health wearables: the conflict between clinical accuracy and everyday wearability. Most devices optimise for one at the expense of the other.

Through twelve weeks of ethnographic research with chronic illness patients and three rounds of physical prototyping, I developed a form language that treats the device as jewellery first and medical instrument second — without compromising sensor performance.

"I stopped wearing my tracker because it felt like wearing my diagnosis on my wrist."
Research participant, Type 1 Diabetic, Age 34
Project 01 — Process 01

Design Process

01

Ethnographic Research

Shadowed 8 participants across 3 weeks. Mapped body-worn device friction points through contextual inquiry and diary studies. Identified three primary failure modes: aesthetic dissonance, interaction interruption, and passive surveillance anxiety.

02

Form Exploration

Generated 40+ thumbnail concepts before converging on three formal directions: the Arc (continuous surface), the Disc (circular face language), and the Plate (flush, minimal). Fabricated foam models at 1:1 scale for wear-testing.

03

Technical Prototyping

Collaborated with electrical engineering students to embed functional PPG sensor arrays into CNC-machined aluminium shells. Validated optical clarity of sensor window geometry against medical-grade benchmarks.

04

Interface Design

Developed a companion iOS app using a single-axis navigation paradigm. Health data surfaces only on intentional gesture — countering the surveillance anxiety identified in research.

Early form explorations — foam models & sketching
Final direction — dimensioned sketch, material callouts

Kite

FinTech UX Design Design Systems

A mobile banking application designed for people who feel excluded by the visual language of traditional finance — the grey gradients, the padlock icons, the small print. Kite is legible, immediate, and treats money as a tool rather than a threat.

The central design challenge was making financial complexity feel manageable without hiding it. Every screen was tested against a dual metric: comprehension speed and emotional response.

Key Design Decisions

Monochromatic palette to reduce cognitive noise. Circular input for amounts — a familiar, forgiving gesture. Transaction history as a continuous log, not a categorised report. One primary action per screen, always.

Project 02 — Design System 02

Typography & Spacing System

Kite uses a strict 8px base unit grid with a 4-step typographic scale. All spacing values are multiples of 4px.

The system is documented in a 48-component Figma library, including dark mode variants and accessibility annotations for all interactive elements.

Vessel

Consumer Electronics Audio Ambient Computing

Vessel is a study in restraint. The brief was to design a smart home audio device that disappears when not in active use — that becomes part of the room rather than a declaration of technology.

The form draws from ceramic vessel traditions: the organic taper, the weighted base, the material humility. The interface is touch and voice — no screen, no glowing logo, no visual noise. Presence is indicated only by a single light ring at the crown.

"Good design, when it works, is invisible."
Dieter Rams — cited as primary reference

IDSA Student Merit Award — Consumer Electronics Category, 2023. Shortlisted for Core77 Design Award.
