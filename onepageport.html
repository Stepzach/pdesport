Who I Am

About

I'm a third-year student at RISD studying Industrial & Product Design with a concentration in interaction design. I care about the kind of design that quietly improves someone's day — not the kind that wins awards for looking interesting.


Before design school, I spent a summer doing field research in rural Taiwan looking at how elderly farmers interact with mobile banking — which pretty much rewired how I think about assumptions. Now I try to start every project with as little assumed as possible.


Outside of school I'm running an editorial design blog, making embarrassingly niche playlists, and slowly learning to be a mediocre climber.

How I Work

Process

01

Understand

I start by listening more than talking. Interviews, shadowing, reading — I want to understand the actual problem before touching a frame.

02

Synthesize

Affinity mapping, journey mapping, picking a clear POV. I try to write the design brief like a journalist, not a marketer.

03

Make

Sketch before Figma. Paper prototypes before pixels. I prototype the assumption, not the solution — there's a difference.

04

Refine

Test early, test often. I treat critique like data, not personal feedback — and I'm getting better at the distinction every semester.
