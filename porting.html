Air Liquide

A redesigned protective enclosure for high-pressure gas cylinder valves, balancing impact resistance, serviceability, and manufacturing cost.

1
Valve Guard Design
Context

Context

Air Liquide's existing valve guards were prone to cracking under repeated warehouse handling, leading to costly cylinder recalls and safety flags across distribution sites.

The brief called for a redesign that could be retrofitted onto the existing cylinder neck without changing upstream manufacturing tooling.

6 wksProject length
40+Field units tested
2
Valve Guard Design
Problem

Problem

Drop testing revealed the failure point was concentrated at the guard's base weld, where repeated micro-impacts propagated stress fractures over time.

Any fix needed to reduce peak stress at that joint while staying within existing weight and cost targets set by procurement.

3
Valve Guard Design
Development

Development

Iterative FEA modeling guided rib placement across twelve prototype geometries, each printed and impact-tested at three drop heights.

The final geometry moves load away from the weld seam entirely, distributing impact energy through a ribbed shell instead.

Outcome
