Français

Le Phals-
bourg

Authentic and very high quality french cuisine, everything is sourced in france. Menu is set on a chalkboard every day based on what they have.

10/10 dinner. We had a côte de bœuf sharing platter. Expect to spend at least 30€ pp.

3 Rue de Phalsbourg 17ème ArrondissementArr
METRO: Monceau & Malesherbes
Bouillon, Français

La Cantine Lourmel

A cheap but good restaurant with classic french bistro dishes. Gets busy at lunch time (and probs for dinner too). First-come-first-served, no reservations

I've been here a few times, its cheap, friendly and good food. What more do you want?

7 Rue de Lourmel 15ème ArrondissementArr
METRO: Dupleix
Onigiri Bar

Komé Komé

Small boutique that sells speciality onigiris and inaris at reasonable prices and for all tastes

Gets busy around lunchtime, so expect a queue, and take away to eat somewhere quieter

Season Marais

Excellent breakfast & lunch place for Pancakes, Eggs, Avocado, Salads... Bit touristy but good food.

No reservations. The terrace is perfect for when its nice outside

Quiet and cosy restaurant with a very friendly owner. Authentic and tasty food & drinks

Sports Bar

Rosie's Smoke
house

Not a typical parisian bar (and is kinda touristy) but I like it because it's central, the pint is 5€ all night and they have a pool table.

They put the Arsenal game on the TV when I asked (don't try this if PSG is playing)

13 Rue de la Huchette 5ème ArrondissementArr
METRO: Saint-Michel
Outdoor bar

Bonnotte Club

An open-air terrace right on the Seine, next to the Eiffel Tower, but suprisingly not too touristy. Young crowd and gets very busy from 6pm when the weather is nice.

Drinks are reasonably priced (for the area). NOTE: Closed in the winter!

Cheap Bar

Les Affranchis

Another nice little bar with a terrace and cheap drinks. Pretty good central location and closes at 2AM on the weekends

28 Bd Voltaire 11ème ArrondissementArr

Sports Bar

The Long Hop

Top bar to watch sports. They have a decent terrace with screens, and many more screens + large projector inside. Gets very busy for big events, I advise you go early

They have beer pitchers from €16, and a HH from 16h to 20h (so maybe even less, not sure)

A games bar with billiards & darts. Really lively and young atmosphere and nice decoration

Cheap Bar

Le Nova

Another cheap bar with terrace and a good atmosphere in the evenings. Good location to bar hop, as there are many other bars around.

€ 3.5 pint during happy hour (17h - 23h)

82 Av. de Clichy 17ème ArrondissementArr
METRO: La Fourche
Sunset / Evening

Canal Saint-Martin

Young and energetic area but has some peaceful side roads. After work, people sit and have drinks/apero on the canal.

Reminds me of Camden. If it's hot you can watch kids jump from bridges.

Canal Saint-Martin 10ème ArrondissementArr
METRO: République, Jaurès
Open-air market

Les Puces de Saint-Ouen

The world's largest flea market, technically several markets bunched together. There are some really nice parts with serious antiques & vintage clothes.

Some areas are not as nice, but as long as you stay near the main area, you will find some cool stuff

Saint-Ouen 18ème ArrondissementArr
METRO: Porte de Clignancourt
Afternoon walk

Jardin du Luxem-
bourg

The Sénat building with a fountain & formal gardens, tennis courts, playground and petanque. The perfect park for all.

Metal chairs are scattered all around the park, for anyone to use.

Jardin du Luxembourg 6ème ArrondissementArr
METRO: Odéon & others
Weekday walk

Parc Monceau

A Smaller and quieter park in one of the nicest areas in paris, surrounded by grand haussmannian buildings.

English-garden layout and a pond. Definetly go walk around outside of the park too

Parc Monceau 8ème ArrondissementArr
METRO: Monceau
June 21st
(Every Year)

Fête de la Musique

Musicians of all levels perform free concerts in streets, parks and squares. Expect large crowds in central areas from 4pm onwards.

Around République and Bastille the party goes on until the morning hours.

Everywhere
METRO: Some stations may be closed
Late Febuary
9 Days

Salon de l' Agriculture

France's biggest farming show, hundreds of animals brought into the city, plus every region's food and drink offered in hundreds of stands.

People go to eat and drink all day with their freinds, there's music aswell. Highly recommend.

Porte de Versailles 15ème ArrondissementArr
METRO: Porte de Versailles or Balard
Mid May
City-wide

La Nuit des Musées

Museums across Paris open late into the night, most for free. Plan ahead to see where you wanna go. Around the 2ème arrondissement there are many options

Super fun, I recommend having dinner / drinks before and then just walk around.

City-wide
METRO: Varies
July 14th
National

Bastille Day

Military parade in the morning down the Champs-Élysées and in the evening there's a fireworks display over the Eiffel Tower.

If you can, go up on your roof to watch the fireworks, otherwise anywhere near the Eiffel Tower will do!

Champs-Élysées & Champ de Mars
METRO: Varies
London
to Paris

Eurostar Snap

Discounted last-minute tickets for trains that still have space (not only for london infact). You pick a date, and the exact train time is confirmed 48 hours before.

Super useful if you don't care what time you arrive, tickets are usually around £30

Book online snap.eurostar.com
