My favourite Restaurants

A mix of classic french cuisine, cool & fun restaurants i enjoyed, cheap kebab places and some simply with a nice atmospheres.

Le Phalsbourg

français 30-70€ 17eme Arr

Authentic and very high quality french cuisine, everything is sourced in france. Menu is set on a chalkboard every day based on what they have. We had a côte de bœuf sharing platter, with roasted potatoes and a side salad. We also had really nice starters and a red wine recommended by the server.

10/10 dinner but expect to spend at least 30€ pp without the wine

Best areas to walks

Paris is the best city to wander in, because its not big and everything is beautiful, especially if the weather is nice. Here are my favourite walks and why:

Along the Canal Saint-Martin

Best time: Sunset / Early evening 10eme Arr

Young and energetic area but has some peaceful side roads. After work, people sit and have drinks/apero on the canal (especially in the summer). If its very hot you can watch kids jump from the bridges.

Reminds me of camden / primrose area in london.

vibes ★★★★★ (5/5 in the summer)

Metro: République, Jaures & others

Les Quais de la Seine

Best time: Anytime, golden hour is nicest

The riverside itself — the stretch through central Paris is UNESCO-listed, and much of it is closed to cars (Voie Georges Pompidou / Les Berges). Good for a straight-line walk between landmarks: Musée d'Orsay, Pont Neuf, Notre-Dame, Île Saint-Louis.

vibes ☆☆☆☆☆ (add your rating)

Metro: anywhere along lines 1, 4, 7, 10, or the RER C

Jardin du Luxembourg

Best time: Morning, before it fills up 6eme Arr

Paris's most "Parisian" park — formal gardens, the Medici fountain, tennis courts, and the Sénat building. Good for a slow lap with a coffee, or bring a book and grab one of the green metal chairs by the pond.

vibes ☆☆☆☆☆ (add your rating)

Metro: Odéon, or RER B Luxembourg

Parc Monceau

Best time: Weekday mornings 8eme Arr

Smaller and quieter than Luxembourg, with an English-garden layout — follies, a fake ruined colonnade, a pond. Surrounded by grand haussmannian buildings, so it doubles as a nice detour if you're near Monceau or the 17th.

vibes ☆☆☆☆☆ (add your rating)

Metro: Monceau

Les Puces de Saint-Ouen

Best time: Sat–Mon, mornings Saint-Ouen (just north of the 18eme)

The world's largest flea market — technically several markets stitched together, from serious antiques to vintage clothes and pure junk. Less a "walk" and more a maze; give it at least 2 hours and expect to haggle.

Metro: Porte de Clignancourt

Yearly Events in Paris / France

The best events you can't miss, and how to prepare. in chronological order

Fête de la musique

21st of June Nationwide party All day & night

country wide festival where musicians of all levels perform free concerts in streets, parks and squares with special events organised all across paris. Expect large crowds in central areas from 4pm onwards. I recommend to check social media for specific artists, otherwise simply walking around you will find concerts on every street.

Around republique and bastille the party will go on until the morning hours. Its like notting hill carnival but bigger and for all types of music.

vibes ☆☆☆☆☆ (add your rating)

Everywhere

Le Salon de l'Agriculture

Late Feb – early March 9 days Porte de Versailles

France's biggest farming show — hundreds of cows, sheep, horses and goats brought into the city, plus every region's food and drink under one roof. Sounds niche, is genuinely great: go hungry and taste your way around the regional pavilion.

vibes ☆☆☆☆☆ (add your rating)

Metro: Porte de Versailles (line 12) or Balard (line 8)

La Nuit des Musées

Mid May One night City-wide

Museums across Paris (and France) open late into the night, most for free. A good excuse to finally do the Louvre or Musée d'Orsay without the daytime crowds — smaller museums often put on talks, music or performances too. Popular spots still queue up, so pick 2-3 you actually want to see rather than trying to do everything.

vibes ☆☆☆☆☆ (add your rating)

Paris Marathon

Early April One Sunday Champs-Élysées to Avenue Foch

Starts on the Champs-Élysées and winds through the city past most of the big landmarks before finishing near the Arc de Triomphe. Great free spectator sport even if you're not running — good vantage points along the Seine or near Bastille where the crowd noise is loudest.

vibes ☆☆☆☆☆ (add your rating)

14 Juillet (Bastille Day)

14th of July Morning parade + evening fireworks

The national holiday. A military parade goes down the Champs-Élysées in the morning (arrive early if you want a spot near the barriers), and in the evening there's a big fireworks display over the Eiffel Tower — the Champ de Mars and Trocadéro both fill up hours in advance, so a picnic further back along the Seine is a less stressful way to watch.

vibes ☆☆☆☆☆ (add your rating)

also worth knowing about

  • Journées du Patrimoine — third weekend of September. Buildings normally closed to the public (embassies, ministries, private mansions) open their doors for free, one weekend a year.
  • Nuit Blanche — first Saturday of October. An all-night contemporary art event, with installations and performances popping up across the city until sunrise.
  • Roland Garros — late May into early June. The French Open, held at Stade Roland Garros in the 16th — grounds passes (no show-court seat) are a cheaper way to soak up the atmosphere.

Practical notes

The boring-but-useful stuff. Stuff I wish someone had told me before I got here.

getting to paris cheap: eurostar snap

  • Eurostar Snap sells discounted last-minute seats (up to ~50% off) on trains that still have space — you pick a date and a morning/evening window, not an exact train
  • your exact train time is only confirmed 48 hours before departure, so it only works if your schedule is flexible on the day
  • bookable up to 14 days ahead (down to the day before, if seats remain); tickets are non-refundable and non-changeable
  • tuesday/wednesday travel tends to have the cheapest fares — weekends are the most expensive and least likely to have Snap availability at all

day trips out of paris

Versailles

The obvious one, and worth it: the palace and gardens are enormous. RER C direct from central Paris (~40 min). Go early or you'll spend half the day queuing; the gardens are free to wander without a ticket.

Fontainebleau

A quieter, less crowded alternative to Versailles with its own huge château, plus a forest around the town that's great for a walk or a picnic if the weather's good. Train from Gare de Lyon (~40 min), then a short bus to the château.

Rouen

A proper day out rather than an afternoon: a well-preserved medieval old town, a Gothic cathedral (the one Monet painted repeatedly), and the site where Joan of Arc was executed. About 1h15 by train from Gare Saint-Lazare.
