Tips
Français, 30-70€

Le Phalsbourg

Authentic and very high quality french cuisine, everything is sourced in france. Menu is set on a chalkboard every day based on what they have.

10/10 dinner. We had a côte de bœuf sharing platter. Expect to spend at least 30€ pp.

3 Rue de Phalsbourg, 75017 Paris
METRO: Monceau & Malesherbes

Bouillon, Cheap

La Cantine Lourmel

A "bouillon" style cantine — classic french bistro dishes (think croque, tartare, roast chicken) at old-school prices, in a big lively room.

First-come-first-served, no reservations, so expect a short wait after 8pm.

7 Rue de Lourmel, 75015 Paris
METRO: Dupleix

Sports Bar, 5e Arr.

Rosie's Smokehouse BBQ

Not a typical parisian bar (and is kinda touristy) but I like it because it's central, the pint is 5€ all night and they have a pool table.

They put the Arsenal game on the TV when I asked (don't try this if PSG is playing).

13 Rue de la Huchette, 75005 Paris
METRO: Saint-Michel

Guinguette, 7e Arr.

Bonnotte Club

A barge + open-air terrace right on the Seine, foot of the Eiffel Tower. Cocktails, oysters, smash burgers, sunset views.

Runs seasonally (spring–autumn) — check it's open before you go.

Port de Suffren, 75007 Paris
METRO: Bir-Hakeim / RER C

Happy Hour, 15e Arr.

L'atelier Suffren

No-frills neighbourhood bar facing UNESCO, a short walk from the Eiffel Tower. Big terrace, cheap pints during happy hour.

Screens for football and rugby.

110 Avenue de Suffren, 75015 Paris
METRO: Ségur / Sèvres-Lecourbe

70s Vintage, 13e Arr.

Les Affranchis

Bar-brasserie with a retro industrial look — wood, vintage furniture, an all-you-can-eat sweet & savoury brunch buffet on sundays.

Also does happy hour and sports screenings.

98 Rue de Tolbiac, 75013 Paris
METRO: Olympiades

Sunset, 10e Arr.

Canal Saint-Martin

Young and energetic area but has some peaceful side roads. After work, people sit and have drinks/apero on the canal.

Reminds me of Camden. If it's hot you can watch kids jump from bridges.

Canal Saint-Martin, 75010
METRO: République, Jaurès

Sat-Mon, 18e Arr.

Les Puces de Saint-Ouen

The world's largest flea market — technically several markets stitched together, from serious antiques to vintage clothes and pure junk.

Less a 'walk' and more a maze; give it at least 2 hours and expect to haggle.

Saint-Ouen, just north of the 18e
METRO: Porte de Clignancourt

Mornings, 6e Arr.

Jardin du Luxembourg

Paris's most "Parisian" park — formal gardens, the Medici fountain, tennis courts, and the Sénat building.

Good for a slow lap with a coffee, or grab a green metal chair by the pond.

Jardin du Luxembourg, 75006
METRO: Odéon / RER B

Weekdays, 8e Arr.

Parc Monceau

Smaller and quieter, with an English-garden layout — follies, a fake ruined colonnade, a pond. Surrounded by grand haussmannian buildings.

Doubles as a nice detour if you're near Monceau or the 17th.

Boulevard de Courcelles, 75008
METRO: Monceau

June 21st, Nationwide

Fête de la Musique

Musicians of all levels perform free concerts in streets, parks and squares. Expect large crowds in central areas from 4pm onwards.

Around République and Bastille the party goes on until the morning hours.

Everywhere
METRO: All lines

Late Feb, 9 Days

Salon de l'Agriculture

France's biggest farming show — hundreds of animals brought into the city, plus every region's food and drink under one roof.

Sounds niche, is genuinely great: go hungry and taste your way around.

Porte de Versailles
METRO: Porte de Versailles or Balard

Mid May, City-wide

La Nuit des Musées

Museums across Paris open late into the night, most for free. A good excuse to finally do the Louvre without daytime crowds.

Pick 2-3 you actually want to see rather than trying to do everything.

City-wide
METRO: Varies

July 14th, National

Bastille Day

Morning military parade down the Champs-Élysées and evening fireworks display over the Eiffel Tower.

A picnic further back along the Seine is a less stressful way to watch the fireworks.

Champs-Élysées & Champ de Mars
METRO: Varies

Travel Tip, Flexible

Eurostar Snap

Discounted last-minute seats (~50% off) on trains that still have space. You pick a date/window, and the exact train is confirmed 48 hours before.

Tuesday/Wednesday travel tends to have the cheapest fares.

Book online
WEBSITE: Eurostar

Day Trip, 40 min

Versailles

The obvious one, and worth it: the palace and gardens are enormous. Go early or you'll spend half the day queuing.

The gardens are free to wander without a ticket.

Versailles
METRO: RER C direct

Day Trip, 40 min

Fontainebleau

A quieter, less crowded alternative to Versailles with its own huge château and forest great for a picnic.

Train from Gare de Lyon, then a short bus to the château.

Fontainebleau
METRO: Transilien Line R

Day Trip, 1h15

Rouen

A proper day out: a well-preserved medieval old town, a Gothic cathedral, and the site where Joan of Arc was executed.

About 1h15 by train from Gare Saint-Lazare.

Rouen, Normandy
METRO: Gare Saint-Lazare
