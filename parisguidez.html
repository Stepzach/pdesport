My favourite Restaurants

A mix of classic french cuisine, cool & fun restaurants i enjoyed, cheap kebab places and some simply with a nice atmospheres.

Français

Chez Odette

A Sunday roast, every day of the week.

A ten-table family bistro near the Canal that never changed its menu and never needed to. Quarter chicken, dauphinoise, a carafe of red — order it without looking.

Order the roast chicken and skip the rest of the menu entirely.

14 Rue de Marseille, 75010
METRO: Colonel Fabien

Bistrot

Le Comptoir Rouge

Steak frites and nowhere to be.

Standing room at a zinc counter, best onglet in the 11th, and a bill that still surprises you in a good way.

Ask for the onglet medium-rare, never anything more.

ADDRESS: 22 Rue Oberkampf, 75011
METRO: Parmentier

Français

La Table d'Hiver

Soup, bread, and a fire in the corner.

Twenty seats, a menu that changes with the cold, open November through March only. Book two weeks out.

Book by phone — they don't check email until dinner.

ADDRESS: 6 Rue des Quatre Vents, 75006
METRO: Odéon

Boulangerie, 2–6€, 1er arr.

Boulangerie Sainte-Anne

The croissant that ruins all others.

Butter-laminated, first batch out at seven, gone by nine. Go early or go without — there is no third option.

Arrive before 8am or settle for whatever's left.

ADDRESS: 41 Rue Sainte-Anne, 75001
METRO: Pyramides

A short list of places to sit for a while.

Wine bars, late counters, and one very good coffee window. Ordered by how long you'll want to stay.

Bar à vin, 8–14€, 11e arr.

Bar Lucine

A negroni, and a seat by the window.

Small natural wine list, six tables, standing room only after nine. Go at seven if you want to sit.

Sit at the window table if you get there before nine.

ADDRESS: 18 Rue de la Roquette, 75011
METRO: Bastille

Cave à vin, 7–15€, 2e arr.

Cave à Vapeur

A cellar list longer than the menu.

An actual stone cellar under a produce street, natural and orange wines by the glass, a cheese board that counts as dinner.

Ask the cellar master for whatever he's just opened.

ADDRESS: 5 Rue du Nil, 75002
METRO: Sentier

Bar, 4–9€, 20e arr.

Le Zinc de Nuit

Open till the métro starts again.

Regulars' bar, cheap house wine, loud arguments about football. Nobody's checking the time and neither should you.

Cash only, and nobody's counting your rounds.

ADDRESS: 9 Rue des Panoyaux, 75020
METRO: Ménilmontant

Café, 3–8€, 11e arr.

Café Marcelline

Coffee first, then everything else.

A morning espresso counter that quietly turns into an aperitif spot by six, same three staff either way.

Order the same thing twice — morning coffee, evening wine.

ADDRESS: 63 Rue de Charonne, 75011
METRO: Charonne

The routes I take when I need to think.

No map needed, no headphones required. Best walked slowly, and ideally before the city wakes up.

Balade, 45 min, 12e arr.

Promenade Plantée

Paris from the rooftops, on foot.

An elevated garden walkway above the 12th, built into an old railway viaduct. Quiet even in the middle of August.

Enter from Avenue Daumesnil and walk north for the quiet stretch.

ADDRESS: From Avenue Daumesnil, 75012
METRO: Bastille

Balade, 30 min, 7e arr.

Les Berges de Seine

The river, without the cars.

A traffic-free stretch along the water from the Musée d'Orsay to Pont de l'Alma. Best at golden hour, obviously.

Time it for sunset, ideally with something to drink.

ADDRESS: Quai Anatole France, 75007
METRO: Assemblée Nationale

Balade, 40 min, 13e arr.

Butte-aux-Cailles Loop

A village that forgot it's in Paris.

Cobbled backstreets, painted murals, and one very good ice cream stop halfway round. Forty minutes if you don't stop for the murals.

Detour for the ice cream — it's worth the extra ten minutes.

ADDRESS: From Place Paul Verlaine, 75013
METRO: Corvisart

Balade, 35 min, 19e arr.

Canal Saint-Martin, Morning

Before the picnickers arrive.

Footbridges and lock gates, walked before nine while it still belongs to the joggers and the boulangerie queue.

Walk it before nine, while it still belongs to the joggers.

ADDRESS: From Bassin de la Villette, 75019
METRO: Jaurès

Worth clearing an evening, or a whole weekend.

Recurring fixtures rather than one-offs — the kind you can plan a visit around.

Marché, Saturdays, 18e arr.

Marché aux Puces

Antiques, junk, and everything between. Saturdays.

A weekend flea market sprawl on the edge of the city. Come early, haggle politely, bring cash and a spare bag.

Bring cash, arrive by eight, and never take the first price.

ADDRESS: Porte de Clignancourt, 75018
METRO: Porte de Clignancourt

Festival, October, City-wide

Nuit Blanche

The whole city, awake till dawn.

One night a year when museums, courtyards, and ordinary streets turn into an all-night art trail. Wear comfortable shoes.

Pick one neighbourhood and stay in it — the whole city is too much.

ADDRESS: City-wide
METRO: Varies

Festival, June 21, City-wide

Fête de la Musique

Every street corner becomes a stage.

Free live music everywhere, from opera on a balcony to a garage band outside the métro. Just follow the noise.

Follow whichever street sounds loudest and keep walking.

ADDRESS: City-wide
METRO: Varies

Live music, Thursdays, 5e arr.

Jazz at the Cellar Club

A trumpet, a cellar, no seats left.

Weekly late-night sessions in a cramped stone basement that's barely changed since the fifties. Arrive by ten.

Arrive by ten or you'll be standing on the stairs.

ADDRESS: 3 Rue de la Huchette, 75005
METRO: Saint-Michel

Everything that didn't fit a category, and shouldn't have to.

Bookshops, pools, cemeteries, markets — the places that make a Tuesday feel like a good decision.

Librairie, Free entry, 5e arr.

Shakespeare's Corner

A bookshop that lets you sleep in it.

Wandering cats, narrow staircases, readings most Sunday evenings. Go for the books, stay for an hour longer than planned.

Check the events board by the door for that week's reading.

ADDRESS: 37 Rue de la Bûcherie, 75005
METRO: Saint-Michel

Cimetière, Free entry, 20e arr.

Cimetière du Père

The most peaceful walk in Paris.

Cobbled paths, resident cats, and quieter than any park in the city. Bring a map or get pleasantly lost.

Pick up a map at the gate — it's easy to lose your way.

ADDRESS: 16 Rue du Repos, 75020
METRO: Père Lachaise

Piscine, 3–5€, 13e arr.

Piscine Joséphine

A 1930s pool, still doing laps.

Art deco municipal pool with wooden cabins overlooking the water. Mornings only, and only for people who actually swim.

Go before nine; it's a different pool by lunchtime.

ADDRESS: 5 Place Paul Verlaine, 75013
METRO: Corvisart

Marché, 5–12€, 3e arr.

Marché des Enfants

The oldest covered market still standing.

Small stalls, long lunch queues, and a rule that you eat standing at the counter you bought from. Worth the wait.

Eat standing at the counter you bought from, not on a bench.

ADDRESS: 39 Rue de Bretagne, 75003
METRO: Filles du Calvaire
